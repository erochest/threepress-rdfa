About Bookworm

Bookworm is an experimental platform for storing and reading epub-format books online.

What's epub?

Epub is the open e-book standard developed by the International Digital Publishing Forum (IDPF). It is based on a number of other open standards including XHTML, making it ideally suited to be read on web browsers.

Where can I get epub-formatted books?

At this time, contemporary, popular books are not widely available in epub, as neither the Amazon Kindle nor Sony Reader support the format. Feedbooks is a excellent site for downloading public domain and contributed books in epub as well as other digital book formats. Threepress also offers several epub formatted books which have been auto-converted from the TEI XML standard.

What Bookworm isn't

  1. A general ebook platform. There are many good standalone ebook readers for computers, including Adobe Digital Editions, FBReader and Stanza. Bookworm is meant specifically to test the viability of using ordinary web browsers as epub rendering engines, not to host PDF, Mobi, or other formats.
  2. An ebook sharing site. There are currently no plans to support sharing of books on Bookworm. I feel it would be detrimental to the adoption of DRM-free e-books to encourage digital distribution at this critical time. Google App Engine's infrastructure would certainly faciliate this feature, and so it may be considered in the future.
  3. Mature software. Your books may be deleted or altered at any time as features are added or modified. Please store your books locally.

Who is responsible for Bookworm?

Bookworm software is free and available for download under the terms of the BSD license from the download page. It is a product of threepress, a venue for experimenting with open source publishing software, and is written by Liza Daly. Any problems with Bookworm can be reported to bookworm@threepress.org. (Feature requests welcome too!)
