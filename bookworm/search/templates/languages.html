The ePub standard is language-neutral and Bookworm is too. Bookworm search supports advanced full-text features in multiple languages.

Stemming

Bookworm's full-text search feature supports an advanced feature known as stemming. Most European languages indicate part-of-speech by modifying words with prefixes or (more commonly) suffixes. Stemming is the process of reducing all possible parts of speech for a word to a single root, or stem.

In English, for example, 'run' is the most basic or stem form of the verb to run. To pluralize it, we append an –s and get 'runs'. When using the word as a verb in the present continuous, we add –ing to get 'running'. Note that 'runs' can be a noun ("I went out for two runs this week") or a verb in the present tense ("He runs.") From the point of view of a search engine, grammar doesn't matter, just spelling.

A stemmer is software which turns any form of a word — "run", "runs", "running" — into a stem. That means that if you search for one form of the word, all of them will be found. This usually provides a more satisfying search experience.

Changing your search language will not affect which ePubs are searched. Not all ePubs correctly indicate what language they're in, so your search will always be across all of your books, not just the ones in the language you specified. Instead, the language setting determines which stemming rules will apply to your word.

For example, if I set my stemmer to be English and search for 'spaghetti', Bookworm will also search for 'spaghettis', 'spaghetting', 'spaghettied', etc. (Stemmers don't know whether a given stem makes sense; they operate just on the spelling.) However, if I change my language setting to Italian, then 'spaghetti' will stem to 'spaghett' (because –i is a plural marker in Italian, the way –s is in English). A search for 'spaghetti' will find ePubs containing 'spaghetti' whether the ePub is in English or Italian, because the search term itself is always considered.

If you primarily read ePubs in languages other than English, you will likely benefit from changing your default language.

Disabling stemming

If you want to search for a word and have it not stem, enclose it in quotation marks. Quotation marks can also be used to perform phrase searches.
